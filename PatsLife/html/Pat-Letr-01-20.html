1969 June

802 South Locust, Apt. 6M

Champaign, IL, 61820

Dear Mike & Eve,

We are alive at 802 South Locust, apartment 6M, Champaign, Illinois, 61820. I asked Walt if he had anything special to say – he said, ask for money.

After leaving Fort Worth, we went to Wisconsin, then to Champaign, where we’ll be encamped for seven more weeks. The climate here is terrible - it has already rained in the two weeks. We’ve been here – more than El Paso sees in three years. The rain I don’t mind, it’s just the jungle heat – never thought I’d miss the Sun City barf.

Did you know that Mickey Mouse wears a Spiro Agnew wrist watch?

I hope you are all there well.

We left Blacky and Brownie to spend the summer in Fort Worth, and brought only Chewy with us.

Walt’s growing a beard - how did you ever adjust to a beard, Eve? Does it ever get softer?

I hope to hear from you soon. Our telephone number is 356–372 in case you get an urge to call.

Love. Pat.

Pat read books while Walt took preparatory classes at the University of Illinois, prior to one more go at Michigan. Walt was freshly discharged from the Army; he took an early out from the Army for the purpose of going to graduate school. Cutting his enlistment short by two months may have been more important than graduate school. The Viet Nam war had grown more and more objectionable; that Richard M Nixon had ascended to the top of the Chain of Command was simply intolerable.

1970 May

351 B Longshore Dr

Ann Arbor, MI 48104

Dear Mike & Eve,

I hope you’re still at this address – it’s been so long since I’ve written, It’s hard to tell.

Haven’t written cause things stay the same around here. Walt goes to school, I go to work; on the weekends, we drink beer and talk about the studies and the work done during the week. Not very exciting. But we are both glad to be in Ann Arbor – and, good God sometimes we even miss El Paso.

How’s Mike’s progress? Are you in school?

I wish you didn’t have the bad habit of writing only when you’re written to. That’s not fair. How are we supposed to know what’s going on.

We have Chewy and Brownie with us (Blackie’s with the Bronstad‘s). Chewy and Browne get along beautifully together since Browns been “fixed.“ Only thing is that his hair is fallen out in big splotches. Where he has hair, it’s long and shiny, but baldness really doesn’t become him.

We are very, very close to buying a small house of a friend who is leaving. It’s really just a winterized lake cottage on Whitmore Lake (exactly 9.4 miles from campus). It has two bedrooms and looks sort of like this [illustration] Makes your house on Signal Peak [El Paso] look big. Has a dark room in the basement. Has a garage and a nice sized (?) (big compared to house) corner lot with lots of trees – on a lake where swimming, fishing, etc. is allowed.

So if the deal goes through, we cut payments from $150 (rent) to $50 (house payments). Sounds great to us – and no neighbors attacking our eardrums 24 hours a day. {of course though we will have a loan payment of $60 a month, but it’s still cheaper}

Please do write and tell us what’s going on – how the kids are, etc.

Love, Pat

1970 June

362 Grove, Box 192

Whitmore Lk, MI 48189

Dear Mike & Eve,

We didn’t have enough animals before, so we acquired two more. A huge dog and an almost as huge cat came with the house we bought. Boo the dog, is not a very good watchdog, but she really attacks the scraps from the table and any crumbs she finds while she’s running around the house ant-eater style. Pi can’t get used to being the cat around here. She only comes around at meal time and she eats very fast, grumbling the whole time.

We’ve been here almost 2 weeks now – have planted even more flowers. The tulips, violets, hyacinth, and daffodils are in bloom, it is really quiet and peaceful here though we hardly been around much. We just got back from Washington. [D. C.]

The mood there this time was very subdued, almost apathetic. I guess no one really had any confidence that the protest would do any good, but just couldn’t think of any other method. We came away more depressed than ever.

The primary in Texas was really a shock – though I guess it shouldn’t have been. It sounds as if Michigan will be just as well represented as you – I have a real fear that Mrs. Romney will win over Senator Hart. Add her to Griffin – and Gerald Ford – now I’m really depressed!

Right to tell us how you’re doing, etc.

Love, Pat

1971 July 15

362 Grove, Box 192

Whitmore Lk, MI 48189

Dear Mike and Eve,

Thank you for the letter and picture – we enjoyed hearing from and seeing you.

Things are pretty dull around here although we have had an enjoyable summer so far – mostly [illegible ]– went camping and hope to go on a canoe trip soon – up around Canada and Minnesota.

Walt is working on a paper which is due toward the end of August. And I’m busy trying to do as little as possible – as usual.

I enjoyed meeting your motorcyclist friend, we had coffee – but Walt was out of town at the time, so I didn’t ask him to stay. I’m sorry Walt missed meeting him. If you get a chance to see the movie – “the Summer of 42“ – we really enjoyed it. I think you would too.

How did the local election turn out in Fort Worth. We had a similar situation, but surprisingly, the local candidate one – even though he had harsh criticism during his previous term and was considered the underdog incumbent that he was. And how do you feel about politics in general after the recent exposé? we saw Daniel Ellsberg on Dick Cavett and enjoyed that. I kind of feel – what the hell does it matter? Who wins on what platform – does it make any difference. Restore my faith!

Almost forgot – Walt ran into Paul Grosser who is here attending a poli-sci seminar. He’s teaching at LSU now – said Jim Peterson went bankrupt.

Walt just talked to him briefly – and hasn’t seen him since, but Paul is planning on spending the summer here – his wife is to join him shortly. We’ve talked about inviting them out – but I don’t know if we will – even want to. it would be sort of a revival of many things pleasantly or finally forgotten. Sorry I write so seldomly – do let us hear from you

Love Pat

PS Marilyn Butler may visit us with her new Jewish beau.

1971 December

362 Grove, Box 192

Whitmore Lk, MI 48189

Dear Mike and Eve,

No – it’s not a Christmas card – I don’t get around to those until about the second week in January. Nothing new, just cold and wet, dismal, dreary. Chewy did manage to become alert enough to have 6 kittens - that little thing. They are nice and healthy – now we have a total of ten animals around here. What every family needs.

We won’t make it to Texas for Christmas, but might be there around the end of February. Not sure yet. Walt’s sister is still traveling around Europe – Enjoying every minute of it. She sent us a six person plate ((?)) (Looks like a bowling ball mold) from Morocco and something from Greece, which hasn’t arrived yet, and with Bette’s taste, I’m not looking forward to its arrival. She was met at the train station in a Yugoslavia Bordertown., Taken to a luxury hotel to a flower layden room, then they found out she wasn’t who they expected – an Australian ping-pong player!

How are things at your house? Better I hope. Hope to hear from you soon. Love Pat

Dear Mike and Eve,

Thanks for writing – Julie is really growing up since we’ve seen her – and very pretty too. I’ve been intending to write, but never sit down and do it. We do have some news – I’m pregnant. Spring is here. Finally our tulips are coming up and the last of the leaves are falling off the trees. it was in the 60s today. I hate to go to the office on days like this.

I don’t know when we’ll be in Texas again. Guess one of these days you’ll just have to pack your things and come up here. Hope to hear from you soon.

Love

1972 June?

362 Grove, Box 192

Whitmore Lk, MI 48189

Eve, thanks so much for sending all those clothes. I can really use them. It was very thoughtful of you. Except for about with some kind of virus, I’ve been feeling just great – getting fat and lazy. 1973 June 01

362 Grove, Box 192

Whitmore Lk, MI 48189

Dear Eve,

Sorry I haven’t written – first. I was waiting for some pictures to send, then Walt’s parents came, and so on and on.

As you can see, Philip is really growing and getting into everything. He’s eight months now, has four teeth and crawls, stands, and is a lot of fun –. When we visited you at Christmas, Mike wondered who would adapt to who’s schedule – Phillip won by lots more than a nose.

Walt‘s in kind of a slump now – not doing too much work (if any) on his dissertation. His present job ends in June and he’s having trouble lining up another one that’s even halfway acceptable to him.

Do you still plan to visit us? I hope so. we are hoping to go camping in Canada sometime this summer – why not join us? Have you been watching the Watergate hearings?

We think of you too a lot – and hope to see you soon. Love, Pat

We have not gone to a counselor yet – Walt’s still seeing his shrink. Our situation zooms up, goes down, stays in the middle, moves up, etc. I really don’t know what our problems are – many at times – few at times. But we still keep working – and I hope we continue to work together. From conversations with friends, it seems our situation is certainly not unique – but that doesn’t make it better. It’s hard to verbalize thoughts sometimes - to each other – and you in this letter – that’s why this is rather badly written – but it’s nice to be able to feel free to write about to you. Thanks.

Love, Pat.

1973-06-01P2E

1973 December 6

362 Grove, Box 192

Whitmore Lk, MI 48189

Day of swearing-in of new V– P and also day Walt baked cookies

Dear Eve,

Thanks for the letter and picture of Julie - she looks more and more like you. Your greenhouse sounds great – I love plants and would love to fill the house with them – doing pretty well with what little light is available. Send pictures.

I’m not opening a daycare center – the regulations are too involved. What I’m doing is just caring for several kids here in the house. The state legislature has just approved new regulations pertaining to childcare centers so maybe around April I can get involved in a center.

Yes, Philip is walking all over – and he just loves his independence. he walks and talks all the time – babbles really. Besides mama and dad, his first word was shit. He really knows when to use it too – just like his old mom. He surely is a good, sweet little boy (I am not prejudiced!) and his appetite never fails him. We really enjoy him. We took him to a party with us the other night and he simply charmed everyone. Heard enough?

Walt is taking courses and working in the evenings as a security guard. The pay is terrible, but he can use almost all the time studying.

I’m sending you a box for Christmas – something for the kids – and I hope – your long promised robe. I’m really having trouble finding a metal zipper that doesn’t open both ends. In the box will also be Mike’s scripts – I really feel bad about keeping them so long. Will he forgive us? Thanks for sending in the recommendation – I think since I don’t know what you said.

We miss you too. It gets so damn cold up here. Fort Worth is even sounding good – especially with fuel shortage. Walt says hello, Phillips says shit.

Write.

Love, Pat

1974 April 10

362 Grove, Box 192

Whitmore Lk, MI 48189

Dear Eve,

I really had a great time at your house. Wish I were back. Remember that evening after the rat turds – when everything was literally rosy and crazy. It never really did change – and that’s what I remember – being in your house, feeling calm, relaxed, and really happy. i’m really lucky to have friends like you and Mike – just too bad you were so far away. Also, let me know which Sunset books you guys want cause we have quite a few on gardening that we could send you. love Pat.
