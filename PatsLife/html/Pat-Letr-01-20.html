Ordered oldest to newest, in four sets of approxiately 20. Letters are on the left side, notes of context on the right.

Mike Fox and I (Walt Bronstad) knew each other since 1958, working at the Fort Worth Star Telegram Saturday nights. "Slipping papers".

Our friendship continued. Mike became my best man at my marriage to Pat. Our wives became even better friends, corresponding more regularly.

1963 September

1439 University Tce., Apt 837

Ann Arbor, MI 48104

Arrived here [Ann Arbor, MI] today. We are crowded into a one room apartment. Had a very nice trip and will write later.

Pat & Wally

Letter Image

Pat and I ventured to Ann Arbor where I intended to continue an academic career. I had just graduated UT Arlington; aided with an NSF Fellowship the University of Michigan.

Mike had been drafted into the U. S. Army. He was nearing the end of his enlistment; he and Eve were living in Tacoma, Washington, and soon to have their first child, Julie.

The flat we lived in was part of married student housing. It was a disappointingly small, cramped, almost industrial, and pricey, compared to what we had known in Fort Worth and Arlington. For a brief time, early winter, Pat's Mother Clara came to visit. I don't remember how we coped on the minimal furniture we had - the sofa became our bed. I must have spent a lot of time away studying at the library then sleeping in a chair.

Five years and nine months passed between the 1963 September and the 1969 June letters. In that interval Pat and Walt have lived in different places, under different exigencies, sometimes apart.

Pat and I were of light weight age (under 30) in a time of extended cultural turbulence. We were more likely to be tossed about more extremely. By the '57 shock of Russian superiority in space, by the crescendo callout of American hypocricy by the corrosive, self-defeating, miscalculations over Viet Nam.


Sputnik enseminated, if not fathered, The National Defense Education Act. Among the first breaths at birth of that act were funds to support STEM education. I graduated high school in 1959 and almost immediately qualified for a work study program with Chance Vought Aircraft in Grand Prarie paired with Arlington State College in Arlington, TX. My Cousin Gorlyn got a better work study gig with Consolidated Aircraft and SMU. His Father, Homer, had better academic-Work Study connections.


I could do work study Electrical Engineering, but it was grueling and monotonous. I switched to Psychology with perhaps too much encouragement by an academic adviser. Pat and I were pained by the racial hypocricy of the South in which we lived. I applied to midwestern schools, naïevely believing the midwest free of racism. We landed in Michigan, cushioned by ADA money. Unfortunately the fantasy of an academic career tarnished even in the undergraduate atmosphere, Michigan Graduate School did not burnish the fantasy.


We moved on to Chicago, Pat went to Chicago Circle, I worked at the SSA Payment Center as a journeman Claims Authorizer. Days before my 26th birthday I was drafted into the U. S. Army. Pat wanted to continue her education, I opted to give the Army 9 more months of my life to become an officer; with officers' pay we could handle tuition.


Neither Pat nor I adapted well to Army life. I could get an early out if I did so to go to graduate school.

1969 June

802 South Locust, Apt. 6M

Champaign, IL, 61820

Dear Mike & Eve,

We are alive at 802 South Locust, apartment 6M, Champaign, Illinois, 61820. I asked Walt if he had anything special to say – he said, ask for money.

After leaving Fort Worth, we went to Wisconsin, then to Champaign, where we’ll be encamped for seven more weeks. The climate here is terrible - it has already rained in the two weeks. We’ve been here – more than El Paso sees in three years. The rain I don’t mind, it’s just the jungle heat – never thought I’d miss the Sun City barf.

Did you know that Mickey Mouse wears a Spiro Agnew wrist watch?

I hope you are all there well.

We left Blacky and Brownie to spend the summer in Fort Worth, and brought only Chewy with us.

Walt’s growing a beard - how did you ever adjust to a beard, Eve? Does it ever get softer?

I hope to hear from you soon. Our telephone number is 356–372 in case you get an urge to call.

Love. Pat.

Pat read books while I took preparatory classes at the University of Illinois, prior to one more go at Michigan. I was freshly discharged from the Army; I took an early out from the Army for the purpose of going to graduate school. Cutting my enlistment short by two months may have been more important than graduate school. The Viet Nam war had grown more and more objectionable; that Richard M Nixon had ascended to the top of the Chain of Command was simply intolerable.

1970 May

351 B Longshore Dr

Ann Arbor, MI 48104

Dear Mike & Eve,

I hope you’re still at this address – it’s been so long since I’ve written, It’s hard to tell.

Haven’t written cause things stay the same around here. Walt goes to school, I go to work; on the weekends, we drink beer and talk about the studies and the work done during the week. Not very exciting. But we are both glad to be in Ann Arbor – and, good God sometimes we even miss El Paso.

How’s Mike’s progress? Are you in school?

I wish you didn’t have the bad habit of writing only when you’re written to. That’s not fair. How are we supposed to know what’s going on.

We have Chewy and Brownie with us (Blackie’s with the Bronstad‘s). Chewy and Browne get along beautifully together since Browns been “fixed.“ Only thing is that his hair is fallen out in big splotches. Where he has hair, it’s long and shiny, but baldness really doesn’t become him.

We are very, very close to buying a small house of a friend who is leaving. It’s really just a winterized lake cottage on Whitmore Lake (exactly 9.4 miles from campus). It has two bedrooms and looks sort of like this [illustration] Makes your house on Signal Peak [El Paso] look big. Has a dark room in the basement. Has a garage and a nice sized (?) (big compared to house) corner lot with lots of trees – on a lake where swimming, fishing, etc. is allowed.

So if the deal goes through, we cut payments from $150 (rent) to $50 (house payments). Sounds great to us – and no neighbors attacking our eardrums 24 hours a day. {of course though we will have a loan payment of $60 a month, but it’s still cheaper}

Please do write and tell us what’s going on – how the kids are, etc.

Love, Pat

We didn’t have enough animals before, so we acquired two more. A huge dog and an almost as huge cat came with the house we bought. The primary in Texas was really a shock – though I guess it shouldn’t have been. It sounds as if Michigan will be just as well represented as you – I have a real fear that Mrs. Romney will win over Senator Hart. Add her to Griffin – and Gerald Ford – now I’m really depressed!

I’m not opening a daycare center – the regulations are too involved. What I’m doing is just caring for several kids here in the house. The state legislature has just approved new regulations pertaining to childcare centers so maybe around April I can get involved in a center.

Yes, Philip is walking all over – and he just loves his independence. he walks and talks all the time – babbles really. Besides mama and dad, his first word was shit. He really knows when to use it too – just like his old mom. He surely is a good, sweet little boy (I am not prejudiced!) and his appetite never fails him. We really enjoy him. We took him to a party with us the other night and he simply charmed everyone. Heard enough?

Walt is taking courses and working in the evenings as a security guard. The pay is terrible, but he can use almost all the time studying.

I’m sending you a box for Christmas – something for the kids – and I hope – your long promised robe. I’m really having trouble finding a metal zipper that doesn’t open both ends. In the box will also be Mike’s scripts – I really feel bad about keeping them so long. Will he forgive us? Thanks for sending in the recommendation – I think since I don’t know what you said.

We miss you too. It gets so damn cold up here. Fort Worth is even sounding good – especially with fuel shortage. Walt says hello, Phillips says shit.

Write.

Love, Pat

Dear Eve,

I really had a great time at your house. Wish I were back. Remember that evening after the rat turds – when everything was literally rosy and crazy. It never really did change – and that’s what I remember – being in your house, feeling calm, relaxed, and really happy. i’m really lucky to have friends like you and Mike – just too bad you were so far away. Walt still feels like he should stay here and I guess I’m not ready to abandon him – although if I keep being as bitchy as I have been lately, I may be the one who gets left.

After all that spring in Texas, we were greeted almost on the Michigan border, with an ice storm – got stuck on the freeway for a while, etc. etc. It’s been like that since we’ve been back. One day it was nice to stay outside for over an hour without freezing. We stopped at Mammoth Cave in Kentucky on the way back - not as pretty as Carlsbad and I got a rock for Julie - I’ll mail it soon. Also, let me know which Sunset books you guys want cause we have quite a few on gardening that we could send you. Bought this paper cause it reminded me of the needlepoint you were doing – did you finish it? Do write –

love Pat.
